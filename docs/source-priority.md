# Source Priority

## Tier 1: Start Here

These are the best early targets because they appear active, useful, and relatively structured.

### 1. FindAPhD

Why first:

- PhD-specific
- large inventory
- strong domain/category filtering
- often includes funding, deadlines, supervisors, and project framing

Expected difficulty:

- medium

### 2. jobs.ac.uk

Why first:

- strong UK PhD/studentship coverage
- recent active listings
- often includes salary, deadline, and department information

Expected difficulty:

- medium

### 3. AcademicTransfer

Why first:

- good structured vacancy pages
- strong Netherlands coverage
- useful for AI/CS/ML roles

Expected difficulty:

- low to medium

### 4. Inria Jobs

Why first:

- official source
- very strong AI/ML relevance
- pages contain rich structured detail

Expected difficulty:

- low

### 5. EURAXESS

Why first:

- official Europe-wide source
- structured research vacancy pages
- often includes requirements, contact, duration, and funding context

Expected difficulty:

- medium

### 6. Jobbnorge

Why first:

- useful for Norway
- often includes a structured vacancy page or PDF

Expected difficulty:

- medium

## Tier 2: Add After MVP

### Academic Positions

Good coverage, but we should confirm whether the page details are consistently rich enough for our field set.

### DAAD

Important source, especially for Germany, but often more scholarship/program oriented than direct vacancy-first.

## Tier 3: Discovery Only

Use these as lead-generation sources, then verify against the official page before storing as trusted.

- PhDScanner
- OwlIndex
- ScholarshipDB
- ApplyIndex
- ScholarshipPositions
- ScholarshipRegion

## Data Trust Policy

Rules:

- official source beats aggregator source
- if aggregator points to official page, store both
- if only aggregator details exist, mark unverified
- if a record is stale or deadline passed, mark inactive
